Model 10 Hydrocarbon Analyzer

FID Single Channel, Spot Heated Total Hydrocarbon Analyzer

The Model 10 is a microprocessor-based, spot-heated total hydrocarbon gas analyzer designed for accurate, sensitive, and stable measurement.

It uses a flame ionization detector for continuous measurement of a dry sample stream at ambient temperature. The detector is spot heated to help prevent condensation and support repeatable performance across a broad range of hydrocarbon concentrations in gaseous mixtures or ambient air.

Best fit: Single-point total hydrocarbon measurement where the sample stream is dry and ambient-temperature operation is appropriate.

VOC Analyzer

Model 200 Hydrocarbon Analyzer

FID Dual Channel, Methane / Non-Methane / Total, Oven Heated Hydrocarbon Analyzer

The Model 200 is a microprocessor-based, oven-heated methane, non-methane, and total hydrocarbon gas analyzer designed for accurate and stable measurement.

Two independent flame ionization detectors are used: one provides a continuous real-time total hydrocarbon reading, while the second works with a gas chromatography column to separate methane from non-methane components.

The sample is moved through an internal heated pump. A portion of the sample is trapped, separated through the GC column, measured for methane, and then backflushed through the column to obtain the non-methane component. Detectors, sample lines, capillaries, valves, the GC column, and related sample-contact components are maintained in a temperature-controlled oven.

Best fit: Applications that require real-time THC plus separate methane and non-methane hydrocarbon readings.

Model 200/1 Hydrocarbon Analyzer

FID Methane / Non-Methane, Oven Heated Hydrocarbon Analyzer

The Model 200/1 is a microprocessor-based, oven-heated methane and non-methane hydrocarbon analyzer that measures at preset intervals with one flame ionization detector.

After the sample reaches the analyzer through the internal heated pump, a portion is trapped and sent through a GC column to separate methane from the total sample before measurement. After methane is read, the remaining sample is backflushed through the column for the non-methane reading, with results shown on the LCD display.

The detector, sample lines, GC column, solenoid valves, and sample-contact components are maintained in a controlled heated oven to help prevent condensation and support reliable analysis.

Best fit: Methane and non-methane hydrocarbon monitoring where interval-based readings are appropriate.

Model 210 Hydrocarbon Analyzer

FID Oven Heated Methane / Ethane / NMEHC / Total Hydrocarbon Analyzer

The Model 210 is a microprocessor-based, oven-heated analyzer for methane, ethane, non-methane-ethane hydrocarbons, and total hydrocarbons.

It uses two flame ionization detectors. One detector supports methane, ethane, and non-methane-ethane readings after a small portion of the sample is separated through a GC column. The second detector continuously measures total hydrocarbons in real time.

After methane and ethane are read, the remaining sample is backflushed through the column for the non-methane-ethane component. The LCD display reports methane, ethane, residual non-methane-ethane, and total hydrocarbons. Detectors, sample lines, the GC column, valves, and sample-contact components are maintained in a controlled heated oven.

Best fit: Applications requiring methane, ethane, non-methane-ethane, and total hydrocarbon measurement from one analyzer platform.

Dual-Stage Stainless Steel Sample Filter

VIG manufactures a dual-stage stainless steel sample filter for protecting analyzer flow paths from particulate contamination.

The assembly uses a two-piece 316 stainless steel body with 1/8 inch FNPT connections on each end, PTFE seals, and a 316 stainless steel spring.

The first-stage sintered stainless steel element captures particles 60 microns and larger. The second-stage element captures particles 10 microns and larger. Interchangeable elements as small as 0.2 micron are available upon request.

Peripheral

Liquid / Gas Separators

VIG manufactures its own liquid / gas separator for sample conditioning applications where liquid must be removed before the sample reaches the analyzer.

The separator uses a two-piece 316 stainless steel housing, a Teflon membrane, pre-filtration at the inlet, and 1/8 inch FNPT connections at the outlet and drain.

The membrane allows gases to pass while preventing liquid from passing through. Separated liquid falls into an external collection container that can be emptied as needed.

Principle of Operation

A cooling coil lowers the incoming sample gas temperature to room temperature, converting liquids in the gas phase into liquid phase. The cooled sample enters the separator, where the porous Teflon membrane allows gas to pass through while blocking liquid due to surface tension. The dry gas exits the separator for analysis.

Service note: This peripheral helps protect analyzer components in applications where moisture or condensable liquids are present.
